Nokia 3310 screen shader

March 26, 2023

For an internal game jam at Arkadium, we each had to pick a word game idea and develop a prototype in 3 days.

The game I chose to develop was fairly simple, revolving around sending text messages. I got the game loop part done on the first day and spent the rest of my time polishing the screen shader for the phone.

Unity Shadergraph shader

The frame around the screen is from an actual picture of the phone from wikipedia.

Underneath, I used a render texture from a second camera to display a pixel perfect display and then used a shader built with Shadergraph to render it.

I’m not allowed to share the source code, but here’s a breakdown of techniques I used:

  • Bending of the image, using the original screen aspect ratio.
  • Adding a drop-shadow beneath the pixels, since the original screen was not laminated.
  • Adding noise to the solid green background and the drop-shadow to give the impression of dirt and imperfect plastic screen.
  • A vignette to darken the image where the phone shell would have cast a shadow on the screen.
  • Adding a highlight point just outside the screen on the top left, to match the light direction of the picture. It also respects the curvature of the screen added in the first step.

The antenna and battery icons were drawn by me in paint
